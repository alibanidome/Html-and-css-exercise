"ethics of internet use"

Internet ethics encompasses a set of guidelines and moral principles that govern responsible online behavior. These ethics address issues like intellectual property, privacy, security, and the impact of online interactions. They are crucial for promoting a safe, respectful, and beneficial online environment for all user

Respecting intellectual property (IP) means acknowledging and upholding the legal rights of creators and owners of original works. This includes inventions, designs, artistic works, and other creations of the mind. It involves refraining from unauthorized use, copying, or distribution of these works, and giving proper credit when referencing or utilizing them

Protecting privacy is crucial for maintaining personal autonomy, dignity, and safety, and it also underpins a healthy, democratic society. It involves safeguarding personal information from unauthorized access and disclosure, and giving individuals control over their data. This includes taking steps to protect your privacy online, such as using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, and being mindful of the information you share on social media

Using the internet responsibly means being mindful of your actions online and their potential impact on yourself and others.
